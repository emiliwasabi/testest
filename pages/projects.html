A Website is a Room explores the concept of websites as physical spaces, reimagining the digital realm as places with spatial qualities. Each poster visualises the internet as a concept: users, website, network and data. The project questions how we ‘inhabit’ digital spaces, translating digital experiences into environments.

This project was completely produced in VR
(from the creation of the assets to the composition of the posters).

This serie has been submitted and (for the time being) selected
for the 100 Beste Plakate 2025 competition.

Thanks to Guy Meldem who always allows us to create randomly,
even if it looks like nothing at first.

Pépite is a fictitious visual identity for a flea market. It is based on objects found at a flea market and then redesigned to create a catalogue of shapes that define the identity.

A trailer and website were produced to accompany the edition.

Hélène is a website that tells the story of a woman who lived through the horrors of the Second World War. During this dark period in history, women who were accused of collaborating with the enemy were subjected to brutal punishments, one of which was having their hair shorn as a symbol of their perceived betrayal.

This website recounts her story in a more narrative, sensitive and illustrative way. The text is from a letter she wrote that I found in the archives of the International Red Cross.

This visual identity project began with my interest in my digital data.

From social media profiles to forum posts, digital transactions to website visits, our online presence leaves traces that all too often go unnoticed.

What if we could take this data and translate it into an avatar?

By diving into the depths of my digital history, I've gathered together all the information I could find about myself online.

The project comes with two F4, a trailer video, and a little figurine of myself (yes) that can be scanned with a phone.
